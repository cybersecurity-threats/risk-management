Holistic Risk Management Framework

Technical Factors

Focuses on automated safeguards like Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) and AI-driven detection systems which contribute strongly to resilience[cite: 258].

Behavioral Factors

Analyzes psychological triggers like digital impulsivity and "human firewalls," which represent a persistent vulnerability[cite: 151, 498].

Social Media Exposure

Functions as a "risk bridge" where oversharing and peer influence significantly mediate an individual's total cybersecurity risk[cite: 262, 373].

Significant Findings

The Mediation Effect

Our findings confirm that the link between usage patterns and overall risk is heavily influenced by social media exposure[cite: 373].

Perception-Action Gap

Research reveals that even when users perceive a threat, they often struggle to follow correct security procedures[cite: 375].

Youth Susceptibility

Data indicates that young users and frequent social media participants have a higher likelihood of engaging in risky interactions[cite: 271].
