Cybersecurity Threats and Risk Management in Real-World and Social Media Contexts

Empirically assessing how human behavioral factors, modern technical implementations, and social media exposure collectively impact systemic digital resilience.

Study Methodology & Formulation
Formal overview of the research design, operational definitions, and target sample groups

This empirical study addresses a crucial vulnerability in organizational and personal defensive systems by examining the direct impact of human behavioral traits and modern technical protection tools. Within the scope of the Holistic Risk Management Framework, human metrics such as behavioral choices, digital impulsivity, and cognitive biases are analyzed alongside technical parameters to determine their collective influence on establishing resilient perimeters.

A central focus of the analysis is evaluating Social Media Exposure as a significant mediating variable. The research demonstrates that excessive digital oversharing and misconfigured personal privacy settings create an accessible public digital footprint. This exposure actively mediates and shifts the overall risk vector, altering how both individual behavior and technical protection mechanisms translate into actual real-world resilience.

The final operational objective of the model is defining and predicting Digital Resilience, which is measured through three core operational capabilities: proactive threat detection efficiency, incident response execution speed, and comprehensive post-incident loss mitigation. The framework isolates these factors to determine whether strategic interventions should prioritize system automation or target human behavioral conditioning.
