Cybersecurity Threats and Risk Management in real-world and social media

We propose the Holistic Risk Management Framework (HRMF) to study the critical mediation role of social media exposure in individual cybersecurity resilience and behavior.

The Proposed HRMF

Technical Factors

Analyzing the effectiveness of automated defense mechanisms like Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) and AI-driven detection systems in mitigating external threats.

Behavioral Factors

Investigating the "human firewall" through psychological triggers like urgency and curiosity that often lead to password hygiene failures.

Social Media Exposure

Defining the "Risk Bridge" where oversharing and online interaction patterns significantly mediate the link between user habits and total digital risk.

Key Research Findings

Significant Mediation

The research confirms that social media exposure is a primary mediator; high usage and sharing levels directly influence an individual's security adherence.

The Perception Gap

Our study reveals a disconnect where individuals perceive high threat levels but remain unable or unwilling to change their behavior to meet security standards.

Holistic Resilience

The data suggests that technical protection alone is insufficient; true resilience requires addressing behavioral exposure and social media habits simultaneously.
